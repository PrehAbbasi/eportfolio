Artefacts (Unit 1 - Unit 12)

This page collects all twelve unit artefacts from the Machine Learning module. Each card gives a short preview, click "Learn more" under any unit to see the full artefact.

Brief Summary

Based on the artefacts across Units 1–12:

  • Across twelve units I moved from grasping basic ML concepts to confidently building, evaluating, and defending models, growing both technically and professionally along the way.
  • Each artefact captures a genuine snapshot of my learning at that stage, and together they show a clear, honest arc of improvement rather than a polished afterthought.
  • Foundational units like clustering and regression came fairly naturally, while neural networks, autoencoders, and evaluating model performance under real-world noise pushed me hardest.
  • I expected to learn how machine learning models are built, trained, evaluated, and deployed responsibly and that's exactly the journey these artefacts trace.
  • Recurring themes of governance, human oversight, bias, and accountability (from the CrowdStrike outage to generative AI) shaped my understanding of what responsible ML practice demands.
  • In Unit 6 I built and defended an autoencoder model, and in Unit 11 I independently tested how noise and regularization affect AUC and R².
  • The Unit 11 evaluation work reflects far greater technical rigour and critical depth than my earlier Unit 6 proposal, showing real growth over the module.
  • Peer and tutor feedback consistently pushed me to sharpen my critical evaluation, governance awareness, and self reflection throughout the module.
