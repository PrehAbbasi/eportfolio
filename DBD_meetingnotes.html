Meeting Summary (Derived from Email Discussion)

Date:

November 4 - 6, 2025

Topic:

Project Progress Tracker Implementation, Weekly Updates & Task Allocation

Summary:

Following the finalisation of team roles and agreement on communication channels via email, the team leader (Preh Abbasi) introduced a shared Unit 6 Project Progress Tracker to improve collaboration and accountability. The tracker listed each member’s weekly deliverables, deadlines, and space for notes or blockers. Preh emphasized weekly updates every Sunday by 5:00 PM BST and stressed that tasks should only be marked as complete when fully done. He also granted edit access to ensure real-time updating by all members.
Team members Veli Mahlangu and Syed Mubashir acknowledged the plan and praised the tracker’s usefulness. Preh later confirmed new access rights had been granted and a new team member was added.

Key Discussion:

  • A Google Doc-based tracker will be the primary tool for weekly task monitoring.
  • Sunday 5:00 PM BST deadline set for status reviews by the team leader.
  • Comments or blockers must be clearly noted before marking tasks complete.
  • Task allocations will be dynamic and updated based on member availability and contributions.

Action Items:

  • Veli and Syed to begin weekly updates from Week 3 onward using the tracker.
  • Preh to update member access and incorporate Ali once available.
  • All members to inform of any risks to task timelines.

Responsibility:

  • Preh Abbasi: Maintains tracker, assigns tasks, and reviews submissions weekly, write final report for submission.
  • Veli Mahlangu: Contributed inputs, provided dataset, acknowledged tracker structure, agreed to comply with weekly reporting.
  • Syed Mubashir Ali: Acknowledged tracker and began updates on backend system setup, designed pipeline.
  • Ali Lari: Onboarded later and assigned ER-aligned data dictionary review (from Nov 27).

Reflection:

This milestone marked the formal structuring of our team’s project execution. The progress tracker helped translate abstract task discussions into visible deliverables, keeping each member aligned. As the Data Manager, I updated my tasks regularly, including documentation drafts and data cleaning inputs. Preh’s leadership in assigning clear review timelines fostered a sense of responsibility, while the Sunday deadlines gave the project momentum. Weekly updates reduced misunderstandings and ensured all interdependent tasks stayed on schedule. This decision directly supported our ability to meet Unit 6’s submission requirements with improved quality and consistency. It also provided accountability when a new member joined, as tasks could be reassigned and tracked with clarity. Overall, the tracker contributed heavily to timely submissions and collaborative visibility.
