Artefacts

Unit 1 - 3: Initial discussion and Peer Engagement

Artefacts: Discussion Post on IoT Opportunities and Risks

The discussion post examined IoT’s dual nature, highlighting its transformative potential through big data analytics and automation (Marjani et al., 2017), alongside key risks such as data heterogeneity, quality issues, and security vulnerabilities (Huxley et al., 2020). It also stressed the need for scalable architectures and ethical governance, referencing GDPR to ensure compliance.

Reflection:

This artefact reflects my ability to synthesise scholarly literature to critically evaluate both innovation and risk. It set the tone for my engagement with the module and helped me understand the practical implications of data challenges in emerging technologies.

Artefact: Peer Response by Syed Mubashir Ali

Ali appreciated the post’s balance but encouraged deeper exploration into data wrangling challenges and ethical oversight. He introduced ideas like algorithmic accountability and real-time preprocessing, broadening the scope of the discussion.

Reflection:

His critique prompted me to explore ethical design more rigorously, which I later incorporated in Unit 6’s proposal. It also reinforced the value of peer dialogue in refining early conceptual understanding.

Artefact: Peer Response by Veli Mahlangu

Veli highlighted agreement with the post’s coverage of cloud infrastructure and early experimentation, noting the valid industry parallels cited. He emphasised the need for standardisation before analytics.

Reflection:

This feedback affirmed my use of evidence but also revealed gaps in discussing interoperability and technical foundations, elements I later addressed during API schema planning in Unit 6.

Artefact: Peer Response by Ali Khalid Lari

Ali praised the breadth of coverage and offered insightful elaboration on preventative strategies like standardised metadata frameworks and privacy-by-design practices.

Reflection:

This response helped me appreciate governance as a layered strategy rather than just a compliance tool. I later used this perspective to justify design choices in the final group proposal.

Unit 6: Proposal Development and Team Collaboration

Artefact: Final Group Report Submission

The team developed a unified relational database and ETL pipeline to address GreenMart's fragmented retail systems. As Project Lead, I managed collaborative contributions across schema design, data cleaning, pipeline architecture, stakeholder analysis, and GDPR-compliant governance. The final report consolidated sales, inventory, and customer data to improve reporting accuracy and strategic decisions.
